Research Areas

Protostellar Outflows

Investigating jets and wide-angled winds from youngest protostars using high-angular resolution observations. Studying outflow mechanisms, morphology, and their feedback on protostellar envelopes across the mass spectrum.

Astrochemistry

Spatial mapping of ices and molecules in protostellar envelopes using JWST spectroscopy. Exploring chemical inventories and understanding the building blocks of planets in star-forming regions.

Star Formation

Multi-wavelength study of Galactic star formation mechanisms. Examining protostellar evolution, accretion processes, and the role of environment in stellar emergence within filamentary structures.

Protoplanetary Disks

Investigating the formation and evolution of protoplanetary disks around young stars. Understanding disk chemistry, dust evolution, and conditions for planet formation.

Radio Astronomy

Low radio-frequency perspective of protostellar jets and thermal emission using uGMRT. Combining radio interferometry with infrared and optical observations for comprehensive understanding.

Instrumentation

NIR/Optical instrument design and development. Contributing to the Multi-Object Infrared Spectrograph (MOIS) design team focusing on optical and software components.

Key Research Programs

  • JWST Cycle 1

    Investigating Protostellar Accretion (IPA)

    Team member of JWST programs studying protostellar winds, ice chemistry, and feedback mechanisms across the mass spectrum. High-angular resolution observations revealing nested wind structures and spatial ice distribution in protostellar envelopes.

  • JWST Cycle 3

    HEFE: High Angular Resolution Observations of Stellar Emergence

    Investigating stellar emergence in filamentary environments. Understanding how environment influences protostellar evolution and outflow properties in dense molecular clouds.

  • ALMA Cycle 11

    Multi-Scale Protostellar Jet Structures

    Co-investigator on multiple ALMA proposals studying nested jet structures, warm CO molecular backbones of JWST jets, and atomic carbon in protostellar outflows.

  • ALMA Survey

    Orion Protostars ALMA Legacy Survey (OPALS)

    Large-scale ALMA survey of protostars in the Orion molecular clouds. Systematic study of protostellar disks, outflows, and their interaction with the surrounding environment.

  • uGMRT

    Radio Continuum Survey of Thermal Jets

    Principal investigator of uGMRT survey examining low radio-frequency perspective of protostellar jets. Complementing infrared observations with radio interferometry to understand jet physics.

  • Instrumentation

    Multi-Object Infrared Spectrograph (MOIS)

    Design team member for optical and software components of next-generation infrared spectrograph. Contributing to instrument development for future astronomical observations.
